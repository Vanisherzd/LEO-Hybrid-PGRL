Key findings:
1. RMSE remains fixed at 5.35 m across all alpha values, confirming that the mean trajectory prediction is unchanged.
2. The uncertainty-aware policy reduces outage proxy from 5.0% to 1.7% at alpha=0.25.
3. This gives a 3x outage reduction with only 13.8% guard overhead.
4. The best risk-adjusted reward occurs at alpha=0.25.
5. Collision proxy remains 0.000 for all alpha values, so outage is the binding robustness metric in this proxy setup.

Interpretation:
The calibrated uncertainty head is useful for risk-aware control even though it does not improve deterministic trajectory RMSE.
Uncertainty-aware guard adaptation improves robustness by trading a small guard overhead for a large reduction in outage proxy.

Claim supported:
Calibrated uncertainty enables risk-aware LR-FHSS/PGRL control and improves robustness under bounded guard overhead.

Claim not supported:
This does not show improved deterministic RMSE or real hardware packet delivery.
