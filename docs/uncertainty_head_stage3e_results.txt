Stage 3E: Calibrated Uncertainty Head Fine-Tuning

Commit:
1a86294

Summary:
This stage adds an uncertainty-head-only fine-tuning path for the deterministic PGRL checkpoint.
The deterministic mean head is frozen, while only log_var_layer is trained.

Key fixes:
1. Fixed normalized-to-reported-metre conversion:
   err_m = err_norm * DU

2. Initialized log_var_layer.weight = 0.

3. Initialized log_var_layer.bias:
   log_var_layer.bias = log((10 / DU)^2)
   This gives initial per-axis sigma around 10 m.

4. Replaced radial threshold coverage with chi-square Mahalanobis coverage:
   z2 = sum((err_i / sigma_i)^2)

   Cov68 threshold:
   z2 <= 3.506

   Cov95 threshold:
   z2 <= 7.815

5. Added readable axis-sigma summaries.

Validation command:
python -m pytest -q tests/test_pinn.py

python scripts/train_uncertainty_offline.py --epochs 20 --batch_size 256 --checkpoint artifacts/checkpoints/pgrl_deterministic_retrained.pt --output artifacts/checkpoints/pgrl_uncertainty_stage3e_20ep.pt

Validation results:
Tests: 12 passed
RMSE: 5.35 m
<5 m: 61.3%
Initial per-axis sigma: approximately 10.0 m
Epoch 20 axis sigma mean: approximately 3.01 m
Epoch 20 Cov68: 0.625
Epoch 20 Cov95: 0.969

Interpretation:
Because the deterministic mean head is frozen, RMSE and <5 m accuracy are expected to remain unchanged.
The uncertainty head learns metre-scale uncertainty and produces non-trivial calibration metrics.

Notes:
This result should be treated as an uncertainty calibration experiment, not as a new deterministic accuracy improvement.
The paper should not claim improved RMSE from this stage unless the mean head is later unfrozen and retrained.
