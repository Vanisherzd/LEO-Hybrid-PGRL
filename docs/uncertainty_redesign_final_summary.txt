Stage 4:
Risk-aware control ablation.

Policy:
guard_uncertain = guard_base + alpha * radial_sigma_m

Key result:
- alpha=0.00 deterministic baseline:
  outage=0.050, success=0.9500, reward=0.9500
- alpha=0.25 uncertainty-aware policy:
  outage=0.017, success=0.9828, reward=0.9690
- outage reduced from 5.0% to 1.7%
- success improves from 0.9500 to 0.9828
- reward improves from 0.9500 to 0.9690
- guard overhead increases by 13.8%
- collision proxy remains 0.000 in this proxy setup

Supported claims:
1. The model includes an uncertainty-aware output head.
2. The uncertainty head can be calibrated using Gaussian NLL and chi-square Mahalanobis coverage.
3. Post-hoc temperature sweep confirms that the learned uncertainty scale is already near-optimal.
4. The uncertainty head does not improve deterministic RMSE when the mean head is frozen.
5. Calibrated uncertainty enables risk-aware LR-FHSS/PGRL control.
6. Risk-aware guard adaptation reduces outage proxy under bounded guard overhead.

Unsupported claims:
1. Do not claim improved deterministic trajectory RMSE from Stage 3E/3F/4.
2. Do not claim Bayesian neural network unless additional Bayesian methods are implemented.
3. Do not claim real online daily TLE continual learning.
4. Do not claim real hardware packet delivery or LR1121 validation from these stages.
5. Do not claim collision reduction as the main result because collision proxy is already 0.000 for all alpha values.

Paper rewrite guidance:
Use "uncertainty-aware" instead of "Bayesian" unless Bayesian methods are added.
Frame the contribution around risk-aware control, not RMSE improvement.
Describe Stage 4 as a proxy/control ablation, not real hardware validation.
Use conservative wording such as:
